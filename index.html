A Day in the Life... of an artist and teacher learning to code.

Morning Afternoon Evening

8.2.2018

Morning

  • 6:15. am Wake up

    • I feed my dog Margot and my cat Paisley as soon as I get up. I heat water to make tea after feeding them. Then, I brush my teeth, wash my face, and get dressed for the day.

  • 6:45 am. Walk dog

    • I take Margot for a 30 minute walk every morning. She has way too much energy and needs to burn some before I leave for the day. We usually walk in my neighborhood in the morning.

  • 7:15 am. Breakfast

    • After I get back from the walk I eat breakfast which is usually Greek yogurt with honey. I ice and drink my tea that I brewed earlier.

  • 7:30 am. Catch up on work

    • I start my day by responding to emails and planning my day. Afterwards, I will work on projects like this one for Coder Girl or finish job applications. I have spent a lot of time looking, applying and interviewing for new jobs.

  • 10:30 am. Studio visit

    • The Public Relations manager at Craft Alliance stops by to shoot photographs of me working in my home studio for the catalogue being made for the upcoming Artist-in-Residence Anniversary Exhibition. This is a quick visit. I found out that my piece will be on the postcard and the cover of the catalogue.

  • 11:00 am. Lunch

    • I eat lunch before going to work, this week I have left-over quinoa chili. It is still kind of weird to eat my lunch so early, but I have to in order to make it to work on time.

  • 11:15. am Bike to work

    • I ride my bike to Craft Alliance to teach my summer camp. Itâ€™s better for me, my wallet and the environment.

    Afternoon

  • 12:00 pm. Metals camp

    • I get there early to prepare my materials and get everything set up. I am teaching 10-14 year olds how to make metal jewelry and sculptures.

  • 3:45 pm. Bike home

    • The ride home is less fun, but I avoid a lot of traffic. It only takes a like 10 more minutes to bike.

  • 4:30 pm. Pet check

    • When I get home I need to feed my dog and cat and let Margot go outside in the backyard for a while. I usually need to rest for a while after biking home because it is so hot outside.

  • 5:00 pm. More work

    • I continue working on projects and job applications. Soon some of this time can be spent making a sculpture for my next show in January.

    Evening

  • 8:00 pm. Walk dog

    • Margot gets a longer evening walk which is good for both of us.

  • 9:00 pm. Dinner

    • I make a salad with spinach, tomatoes, onions, avocado, and fake chicken for dinner.

  • 10:00 pm. Shower

    • I prefer showering in the evening.

  • 10:30 pm. Work

    • I do more work and web surfing before going to bed.

  • 11:30 pm. Sleep

    • I read the science-fiction novel Codebreakers before falling asleep.
